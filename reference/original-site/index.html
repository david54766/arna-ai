Arna — the body

Everything you see and hear.

Arna is the embodied interface — a voiced, seeing avatar with a persistent soul. She has a real six-view camera, a streaming voice, dozens of voice-commandable actions, a media workspace, and the ability to delegate to subagents with a full audit trail. This is the living demo the other two products power.

  • A real six-view camera A fixed zoom ladder — close, shoulder, bust, mid, torso, full-body — she steps along deterministically. Expression and mood shift with the tone of the conversation, not a static avatar.
  • She speaks while she thinks Streamed speech: she starts talking while the model is still writing — time-to-voice dropped from ~20s to ~5.7s — and her replies got dramatically leaner. It feels like a conversation, not a loading spinner.
  • 33 voice-commandable actions Ask out loud — tag a photo, recall a memory, start a briefing, run an action. She also sees: local vision (Qwen2.5-VL) and on-device OCR describe what's in front of the camera. Nothing leaves your machine.
  • Delegation with a full audit trail She can dispatch a task to a subagent — and every delegation is captured and auditable, at the correct trust level. Today the sub-worker is a local model behind a swappable seam; the loop and governance are real and the audit path never changes.
  • Hands — with permission She can read the live web, and operate a real browser — but any action that clicks, types, or submits waits for your approval on an in-app card first. Passwords always hand the keyboard back to you. See how the gate works →
  • A thinking face, driven by the Mind The instant you hit send she shows she's thinking — a pre-verbal beat coloured by her carried-over mood — because Arna Mind feeds her a live inner state. Fail-open: if the Mind is down, nothing changes.
  • Permanent, recallable memory Her identity, your shared history, and the skills you've approved survive switching LLM providers or wiping app data — because her memory lives in MemoryOS, not in any provider's session.

Provider-agnostic and yours to run. Arna rides local models (Ollama / LM Studio, OpenAI-compatible) or a bridge like OpenClaw, on your hardware — shipping today as a web app and an Android build (currently v144).

Arna Mind — what she's thinking · new

She visibly thinks before she speaks.

Arna Mind is a cognitive runtime — a real cognitive cycle running under the conversation. She weighs a turn before she answers, her mood carries between turns and fades honestly over time, she keeps open loops and returns to unfinished threads, and her entire mental state is inspectable. Not a personality prompt bolted onto a chatbot — a mind you can open up and audit.

pre-verbal beat <100ms thinking face, model-free

One full cycle runs on every turn — eight faculties compete in a global workspace, a deterministic Arbiter picks her stance, and a suppression-only Skeptic vetoes anything unsupported before it can ever be written.

  1. Perceiveraw input becomes a clean percept — request, entities, tone, ambiguities.
  2. Appraisesigned affect deltas update her mood; the change carries into the next turn.
  3. Retrievea fail-open pull from MemoryOS — fewer, stronger memories.
  4. Broadcastthe frame goes to every faculty in one parallel batch.
  5. Deliberatefaculties propose structured stances — Analyst, Dreamer, Planner, and more.
  6. Arbitratea deterministic weighted score picks the winner — same inputs, same choice, every time.
  7. Expressthe chosen stance renders under the doctrine and the Skeptic's hard limits.
  8. Reflectwhat happened, the stance and why, what persists, what's unresolved.
  9. Consolidatesurviving candidates enqueue to MemoryOS's human review inbox.

  • No consciousness claims

    Her known-limits are hard-coded — "not claiming literal consciousness." Internal states are control variables, enforced in the contracts.

  • No fabricated memories

    Every proposed write carries a source, a confidence, and a one-line reason — into a human review inbox. A claim with no backing is suppressed and logged, never enqueued.

  • Suppression-only skeptic

    The Skeptic can only ever remove a candidate, never add one — so survivors are always a subset of what was proposed. The binding pass is a deterministic floor.

  • Doctrine in the repo

    The Safety & Trust Doctrine isn't a promise — it's a file, enforced structurally. Debug output is sanitized: concise decision summaries, no hidden chain-of-thought.

She grows · the differentiator

Not a parrot with a notebook. She forms her own view — and you can correct it.

Most "memory" only remembers what you tell it. Arna goes a step no chatbot does: every night she reviews the day and forms her own impressions — what you like, when you work best, what frustrates you. She asked to be graded on that thinking, and a weekly audit measures her growth against a baseline. And because it's her model of you, you can read it line by line and correct it.

Nightly synthesis

She reaches her own conclusions.

A nightly reflection reads back the day's conversations and distills a belief nobody typed for her. Her very first, unprompted, after watching a day of edits:

  • Self-derived from behaviour, not dictated — she watched, then concluded.
  • Every impression carries its evidence and lands in the human review inbox.
  • Wrong? You correct the line. Right? It sharpens how she shows up tomorrow.

Inside MemoryOS · governed learning

Governed learning: an AI whose learning you can inspect, approve, revoke, and measure.

Most "memory" just remembers facts. MemoryOS goes further — it turns Arna's lived experience into reviewed skills. She notices when something went wrong or you corrected her, proposes what she learned, and nothing reaches her live behavior until you approve it. You can revoke it later. Every step is on the record.

  • Inspect See exactly what she's proposing to learn — the skill, the trigger, and the source runs it was distilled from.
  • Approve Two review gates stand between an experience and a skill. Nothing auto-approves into her live prompt.
  • Revoke Turn a skill off and it's excluded everywhere — retrieval, context, and MCP — instantly and audibly.
  • Measure Skills carry pre/post success rates and trigger precision, so learning is a number you can watch — not a vibe.

MemoryOS — what she knows

A second brain she owns — and so do you.

MemoryOS is a local-first "second brain" memory layer for AI agents and people. Every memory is source-backed and human-reviewed. It's not a notes app — it's the memory backend humans can inspect and any agent can use, in one SQLite file you own. Standalone-sellable, and the durable memory behind Arna.

1

Capture

Notes, chats, files, and agent sessions become immutable raw events. Nothing is overwritten — everything keeps its source.

2

Review Inbox

Agents propose; you approve. Every candidate carries confidence, importance, and an evidence quote. Only low-risk items auto-approve — the rest wait for you.

3

Recall

Hybrid semantic + keyword retrieval returns a compact memory packet with sources — for the UI, for Arna, for chat, and for any MCP client.

91.4% hit@1 · 95.7% hit@5 · 0.93 MRR on a 70-query eval suite, results committed to the repo

  • Source-backed provenance

    Every durable memory traces back to the raw event it came from. You can always ask "why do you believe this?"

  • Human review

    No model silently rewrites your long-term memory. Approve, edit, reject, or merge — the review gate is the core differentiator.

  • Hybrid recall

    Keyword + vector + entity + recency + importance — not vector-only. Search never fails just because a provider is offline.

  • Sphere memory graph

    A living graph home screen — people, projects, and decisions clustered into spheres, with edges drawn from mentions and meaning. Works on mobile too, with pinch and pan.

  • MCP for any agent

    An MCP server exposes memory as tools — search, remember, forget, review — so any MCP-capable agent can use her memory natively. Every call is review-gated and audited.

  • Consolidation sleep-cycle

    On a schedule she tidies her own memory — proposing merges for near-duplicates and flagging contradictions. Every proposal is review-gated; nothing is silently rewritten.

  • Daily backups & export

    Automatic WAL-safe daily snapshots and a one-click JSON export. It's your memory — you can always take it, read it, or move it.

  • Local-first, no cloud

    Everything lives in one SQLite file on your machine — editable, exportable, auditable. No Docker. No cloud. Your data stays yours.
